Impromptu tweetorial at Plant Biology 2015

Have a colleague who should tweet but needs some encouragement?

Drag them to this experimental tweetorial!

This weird take on a tweetup - started as a off-the-cuff comment by @PlantTeaching (Mary) and @JasonWilliamsNY - might be a way to get your non-tweeting colleagues interested and informed. If there is any traction, maybe we can do something more formal next year ??

Tuesday from 11:30 AM - 12PM; iPlant Booth (510)

  1. Share the tweet via word of mouth, email, or other media (this is under organized, so tweeting is the only way I've advertised) - Maybe @ASPB will make this an app notification hint hint :)
  2. If they haven't done so, have your friend/colleague sign up for an account at www.twitter.com
  3. Come by to share your tweet-skillz

My Top 5 Reasons for Biologists to Tweet

  1. Because next to Google, Twitter is one of most important bioinformatics tools
  2. Going to a conference and not being on twitter is like using UCSC with all the tracks turned off
  3. Everyone has the same number of characters - everyone can have the same voice on twitter
  4. People (myself included) who will not reply to your email, but who will answer a tweet!

  5. I don't time for a blog (I'll just retweet yours), I can't be bothered with Facebook, but I'm fine with maintaining an introvert-compatible level of human contact
